# Quiet Engineer

A visual philosophy for the documentation of work that runs unseen.

## Movement

Quiet Engineer is the editorial cousin of infrastructure. It treats a résumé not
as a self-promotional ledger but as a printed page from an imagined trade
journal — a single spread that catalogs a practitioner with the calm authority
of a museum label. Nothing shouts. Everything is placed.

## Space and Form

The page breathes. Generous warm margins frame a centered measure of running
type, with a vertical index of mono-set numerals along the spine like the
foliation of an early printed book. Horizontal hairlines — never thicker than a
single point — divide the page into rooms. Each room contains exactly what
belongs in it. White space is not absence but acoustic: the silence between
movements.

## Color and Material

The page is paper, not screen. A warm ivory ground (oklch 98.4% / 0.005 / 85)
holds near-black ink. A single accent — a restrained amber — appears no more
than three times per page: a small disc near the headline, a thin underline
beneath the role, and a discreet folio. Color is rationed like ink on a
letterpress proof. No gradients, no shadows, no decorative fills.

## Typography

Display type is set in Instrument Serif — its italics doing the work of
emphasis, never weight. Body type is set in a refined humanist sans for steady
horizontal reading. Technical credentials — stacks, periods, identifiers — are
set in a monospaced face at a smaller scale, tracking slightly tightened, as
though pulled directly from a terminal session. The three faces never compete:
serif speaks, sans explains, mono testifies.

## Rhythm and Hierarchy

The composition reads top-to-bottom in a single column, broken only by a
left-rail of section numerals (01, 02, 03 …) and tight three-letter eyebrows
(ABOUT · STACK · ROLE). Headings sit close to their paragraphs; paragraphs sit
close to their evidence. The eye descends through the page as through a flight
of low stairs — steady, predictable, restful.

## Craftsmanship

Every kern is intentional. Every baseline aligns to an invisible 8-point grid.
The hairlines are mathematically equidistant from the type above and below
them. The folio reads like a printer's mark. The piece should feel as though it
came from the desk of a master typesetter who has spent years refining the
trade — the kind of object you might find tipped into a Swiss design annual,
small enough to overlook on first pass and rewarding on the second.

## Restraint

Nothing decorative. No icons. No skill-bars, no star ratings, no progress
meters, no emoji, no QR codes, no headshot. The only ornaments are the
typographic ones inherent to the faces themselves: an italic descender, an old-
style figure, a true small cap. The document trusts the reader.
